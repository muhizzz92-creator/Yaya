The Wood Wide Web

Listen to the Article Click to play

Imagine a social network, but for trees. Scientists have discovered that forests are not just collections of individual trees. They are connected underground by a complex system called mycelium. This is a network of fungi that links the roots of different plants together.

Through this "Wood Wide Web," trees can share resources. A large, old tree (often called a "Mother Tree") can send sugar and water to smaller trees growing in the shade. Furthermore, they can use this network to send warnings. If a tree is attacked by insects, it sends chemical signals through the roots to warn its neighbors to raise their defenses.

Word Focus
Network: A system of connected parts.
Resource: Something valuable like food or water.
Individual: Single; separate.
To Warn: To tell someone about danger.
Defense: Protection against attack.

The City Carved in Stone

‚ñ∂
Listen to the Article Click to play

Petra is one of the most famous archaeological sites in the world. Located in Jordan, it was the capital of the Nabataean Empire over 2,000 years ago. Unlike other cities built on the ground, Petra was carved directly into pink and red sandstone cliffs.

It was a wealthy city because it sat on a major trade route. Traders carrying spices and silk stopped there. However, Petra was eventually abandoned after earthquakes destroyed its water system. It remained lost to the Western world until 1812. Today, it is known as the "Rose City" due to the color of the stone.

Word Focus
Carved: Cut into a hard material to make a shape.
Abandoned: Left empty; deserted.
Wealthy: Rich; having lots of money.
Route: A way or path to get somewhere.

Living on the Red Planet

Humans have always wanted to explore. The next big step is Mars. But living there will be extremely difficult. The atmosphere is thin and contains mostly carbon dioxide. There is no breathable air.

To survive, humans would need to build sealed habitats. We would need to generate oxygen and grow food using high-tech methods. Another danger is radiation from the sun. Despite these risks, many people volunteer to go. They believe it is essential for the future of humanity to become a multi-planetary species.

Word Focus
Sealed: Closed tightly so air cannot escape.
Essential: Absolutely necessary.
Atmosphere: The layer of gas around a planet.
Species: A group of living organisms (e.g., humans).
